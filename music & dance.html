Descriptions about Basotho Traditional music and dances.

Mokhibo

Is a traditional Basotho dance performed mainly by women, danced while kneeling on the ground.

Mohobelo

Is a traditional Basotho dance performed by the men .

Ntlamo

Is a Sesotho dance performed by the boys, during weddings,family gatherings and initiation ceremonies.

Lesiba

is a traditional musical instrument,known for its unique sound and cultural significance.

Mamokhorong

Commonly used in traditional songs and produces soft,melodic tones.

Koriana

A keyboard instrument with bellows,common in famo music,produces rhythmic sound.

Phala

A whistle or flute usually made from reed or plastic,used to entertain.

Sekupu

A drum,often made with animal skin and wood and played with hands and sticks.

Lekope

Played by placing it between the teeth and plucking the metal strip, produces soft tones.

Katara

Borrowed from guitar,a string instrument used in music.

Thomo

It is similar to a musical bow,played mostly by young girls,it is made with string stretched across a stick.

Moropa

A drum ,played during ceremonies,dances and rituals.

Setolotolo

A mouth bow instrument,played by placing one end in the mouth and plucking the string to create a sound.

Nts'oana

A rattle or shaker-like instrument,often made from cans, used to accompany children or women.

Mokhope

A reed or string intrument made from natural materials like plant stalks,used by young boys.

Lilo

Refers to mourning songs during funerals expressed by wailing or chanting by women to show grief.

Phalafala

A traditional horn from an animal, blown to announce gatherings and warnings.

Phoka

It is used in traditional poetry and songs.

Sekhankula

Is a traditional Basotho musical instrument,often handmade and played by young boys,especially herdboys.

Mashoehle

Is generally refers to ankle rattles or shakers used in traditional Basotho dances.

Mokopu

Are the traditional songs that young girls performs during festivals.

Moqoqopelo

It's the part of song that is repeated after each verse and is often the most memorable.

Mothalali

A rare instrument possibly related to voice or percussion

Pitsi

Whistle made from horse bone and wood
